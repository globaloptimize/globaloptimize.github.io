Ming Shi

 

I am a Post-Doctoral Scholar of the AI-EDGE Institute and Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at The Ohio State University, Columbus, OH, USA. I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Ness Shroff and Prof. Yingbin Liang.

I received my Ph.D. degree from School of Electrical and Computer Engineering, College of Engineering at Purdue University, West Lafayette, IN, USA, in 2022. I was fortunate to be advised by Prof. Xiaojun Lin.

Email: shi.1796 [at] osu [dot] edu or sming [at] purdue [dot] edu

I am in the 2023-2024 academic job market for faculty positions.

Research interests

  • Reinforcement learning (security, non-stationarity, safety, partial observability, human-in-the-loop, curse-of-multi-agent), bandit learning and online learning (power-of-2-arms, switching costs, side information), with applications in Edge AI (robotics, autonomous driving, recommendation systems), healthcare, language processing, and generative AI.

  • Online convex optimization (switching costs, prediction, uncertainty), with applications in networking, power systems, cyber-physical systems, wireless communication, date centers, cloud computing, and economics.

  • Theoretical performance analysis, e.g., competitive ratio, regret, sample complexity, and convergence rate.
